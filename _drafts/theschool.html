The school defined We can build a school that allows anyone to reach their full potential in life. This is an introduction to that school. The fundamentals Curiousity, open source resources, and science. Sugatra Mitra has defined an environment that promotes the most productive learning process for groups of children. The main goals are to construct an environment that stimulates curiosity and to allow the children to find solutions to problems without instruction. His experiments show that the optimal system is having groups of four children sharing a computer to solve a difficult problem together. His methods have shown an increase in the standardized test scores and in the retention rates of the children. These are measurable criteria, and the results have been published by Mitra. The Khan academy is an incredible resource for students and teachers. It allows anyone with access to the internet to watch high quality lectures on a multitude of subjects. Children are now free to watch good lectures on their own time and at their own pace, and teachers are free to help students with actual work instead of preparing and delivering lectures. Neuroscience, the science of the brain, has shown us some interesting characteristics of how we learn. Requirements The school is a complete success if : The structures, resources and methods that make up the school are as easy as possible to reproduce: It must be reproduceable. It must consider the physical and mental health of every member using data from scientific studies and social experiments. It must be healthy. It must teach people marketable skills. It must be relevant. It must embrace the fleeting nature of life. It must be fun.
